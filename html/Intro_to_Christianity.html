Introduction to Christianity

Christianity is a monotheistic religion based on the life and teachings of Jesus Christ. It is the world's largest religion, with over two billion followers worldwide. Christians believe in the Holy Trinity, which consists of God the Father, God the Son (Jesus Christ), and God the Holy Spirit.

The Bible is the sacred scripture of Christianity, divided into the Old Testament and the New Testament. The Old Testament contains texts that are also part of the Jewish faith, while the New Testament focuses on the life, teachings, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ.

Christianity has many denominations, including Roman Catholicism, Eastern Orthodoxy, and Protestantism. Each denomination has its own beliefs, practices, and traditions, but they all share a common faith in Jesus Christ as the Savior.

Core Beliefs

  • Belief in one God who is omnipotent, omniscient, and omnipresent.
  • Jesus Christ is the Son of God and the Savior of humanity.
  • The resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead.
  • Salvation through faith in Jesus Christ.
  • The importance of love, compassion, and forgiveness.

My Denomination

More specifically, I am a part of KOSIN, the Korean Presbyterian Church in America.
